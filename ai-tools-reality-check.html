üí° Practical Integration Tips

  • Start small: Use AI for one specific task (like transcription) before expanding
  • Always verify: Treat AI output as a first draft that needs human review
  • Learn the prompts: Specific, detailed prompts get much better results
  • Know the biases: AI inherits biases from training data‚Äîbe especially careful with sensitive topics
  • Keep it ethical: Disclose AI use in your methods, especially for analysis
  • Don't replace thinking: Use AI to handle tedious work, not critical decisions

üéØ Actual Problems to Watch For:

  • Hallucination: AI makes up citations, statistics, and "facts"
  • Bias amplification: Reproduces societal biases in analysis
  • Over-reliance: Students/researchers stop developing critical thinking
  • Ethical gray areas: Unclear disclosure requirements

üõ°Ô∏è How to Mitigate These Risks

The solution isn't to avoid AI‚Äîit's to use it intelligently:

  • Always fact-check AI-generated content
  • Use multiple sources for important claims
  • Be transparent about AI use in your methods
  • Maintain your critical thinking skills‚Äîuse AI as assistance, not replacement
  • Stay updated on your field's AI ethics guidelines

üöÄ The Future is Integration, Not Replacement
