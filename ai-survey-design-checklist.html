AI-Assisted Survey Design Checklist

Use AI to enhance your survey design while maintaining methodological rigor and avoiding bias amplification.

📋 Download Checklist 📝 View Examples

🎯 Why This Checklist Matters

AI can accelerate survey design, but it comes with risks: bias amplification, leading questions, and methodological blind spots. This checklist helps you harness AI's benefits while maintaining the rigor your research demands.

⚠️ Critical Warning

Never use AI-generated questions without validation. AI inherits biases from training data and may create leading questions, cultural insensitivities, or methodological flaws. Always apply your expertise to evaluate and refine AI suggestions.

📋 The Complete Checklist

Follow these phases in order. Each builds on the previous to ensure methodological soundness.

  • Phase 1
    Pre-AI Foundation
    Establish your research framework before engaging AI. This prevents AI from derailing your methodology.
    ✓ Define research questions clearly ✓ Identify target population ✓ Choose theoretical framework ✓ Set measurement objectives ✓ Document assumptions and constraints
  • Phase 2
    Strategic AI Prompting
    Craft specific prompts that provide context while avoiding leading the AI toward predetermined answers.
    ✓ Include research context ✓ Specify target population ✓ Request multiple variations ✓ Ask for different question types ✓ Specify scale requirements
  • Phase 3
    Bias Detection & Validation
    Systematically evaluate AI suggestions for bias, leading language, and methodological issues.
    ✓ Check for leading questions ✓ Identify cultural bias ✓ Evaluate response options ✓ Test reading level ✓ Review for double-barreled questions
  • Phase 4
    Human Validation & Testing
    Apply human expertise and empirical testing to finalize your survey instrument.
    ✓ Expert review ✓ Cognitive interviews ✓ Pilot testing ✓ Reliability analysis ✓ Document AI use in methods

💡 Effective AI Prompts for Survey Design

✅ Good Prompt Example:

"I'm designing a survey to measure job satisfaction among remote workers aged 25-45 in the technology sector. My theoretical framework is based on Herzberg's Two-Factor Theory. I need 5 questions about work-life balance that use a 5-point Likert scale. The questions should be culturally neutral and avoid assumptions about family structure. Please provide multiple variations for each concept."

❌ Poor Prompt Example:

"Write survey questions about job satisfaction."

Why this fails: No context, population, theoretical framework, or constraints. Will likely produce generic, potentially biased questions.

💡 Pro Tips for AI Survey Design

  • Start with concepts, not questions: Ask AI to help brainstorm ways to measure specific constructs
  • Request alternatives: "Give me 3 different ways to ask about [concept]"
  • Specify constraints: Reading level, cultural considerations, length limits
  • Ask for criticism: "What potential biases do you see in this question?"
  • Iterate systematically: Refine prompts based on what works and what doesn't

🚨 Red Flags: When AI Goes Wrong

Leading Questions

Example: "How satisfied are you with our excellent customer service?"
Fix: "How would you rate our customer service?"

Double-Barreled Questions

Example: "How satisfied are you with the quality and price of the product?"
Fix: Split into separate questions for quality and price.

Cultural Assumptions

Example: "How does your spouse feel about your work schedule?"
Fix: "How does your household feel about your work schedule?"

Loaded Language

Example: "How much do you hate working from home?"
Fix: "What is your overall experience working from home?"

📊 Mixed Methods Integration

When using AI for mixed methods surveys, consider how AI-generated questions will integrate with your qualitative components:

Sequential Design Considerations

  • Use AI to generate follow-up questions based on qualitative themes
  • Test AI questions in qualitative interviews before quantitative deployment
  • Ask AI to suggest ways to quantify qualitative concepts
  • Use AI to create bridge questions between quantitative and qualitative sections

Concurrent Design Tips

  • Ensure AI questions don't bias qualitative responses
  • Use AI to generate demographic questions that work for both components
  • Consider how AI-generated scales will translate to qualitative analysis
