Ethics Statement - AI Transparency
Ethical Considerations of AI Use

The integration of artificial intelligence tools in this research was guided by principles of transparency, accountability, and academic integrity. All AI use was disclosed to [SPECIFY: participants, institutional review board, funding agency] and documented throughout the research process.

Transparency Measures:
‚Ä¢ Participants were informed of AI use during [SPECIFY: consent process, data collection, other]
‚Ä¢ All AI-generated content was clearly marked and attributed
‚Ä¢ The specific roles and limitations of AI tools were explicitly documented
‚Ä¢ Research team members maintained oversight of all AI applications

Bias and Fairness: We acknowledge that AI systems may perpetuate or amplify existing biases. To address this concern, we [DESCRIBE SPECIFIC MEASURES: e.g., "tested AI outputs across diverse demographic groups," "used multiple validation methods," "consulted with community stakeholders"].

Academic Integrity: AI tools were used to enhance, not replace, human analytical thinking. All research conclusions, theoretical interpretations, and methodological decisions remained under direct human control. AI suggestions were treated as one source of input among many in the research process.
Limitations Section - AI Considerations
Limitations Related to AI Use

The use of artificial intelligence tools in this study introduces several important limitations that readers should consider when interpreting our findings.

AI Model Limitations: The AI tools used in this study [LIST SPECIFIC TOOLS] are trained on data that may not fully represent the diversity of [SPECIFY: populations, contexts, perspectives] relevant to our research. This may introduce systematic biases that, despite our mitigation efforts, could influence [SPECIFY: data collection, analysis, interpretation].

Transparency Constraints: While we have disclosed our AI use extensively, the proprietary nature of some AI systems means that complete algorithmic transparency is not possible. The specific training data and decision-making processes of [SPECIFY TOOLS] are not fully accessible for independent verification.

Temporal Limitations: AI tools are rapidly evolving, and the versions used in this study [SPECIFY VERSIONS AND DATES] may perform differently than current or future iterations. This may affect the reproducibility of our AI-assisted processes.

Human-AI Interaction Effects: The effectiveness of AI assistance depends partly on how humans interact with these tools. Our team's specific expertise, prompting strategies, and validation approaches may not be easily replicated by other researchers, potentially affecting generalizability.

‚úÖ Best Practices for AI Disclosure

  • Be Specific: Name exact AI tools, versions, and dates used
  • Describe Scope: Clearly explain what AI did and didn't do in your research
  • Document Process: Keep records of AI interactions for potential audit
  • Validate Extensively: Never accept AI output without human verification
  • Consider Context: Adapt disclosure to your field's specific ethical concerns
  • Stay Updated: Monitor your field's evolving AI ethics guidelines

üö´ Don't Do This

  • Vague disclosure: "AI tools were used in analysis"
  • Hiding AI use hoping no one will notice
  • Using AI for data interpretation without validation
  • Assuming AI is always neutral or objective
  • Forgetting to update disclosure as AI use evolves
